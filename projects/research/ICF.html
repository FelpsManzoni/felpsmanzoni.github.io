2. Invitation to Participate

This informed consent form applies to participation in research studies investigating the adoption and use of Software Engineering and Testing practices in the development of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (AI/ML) models and in the integration of AI/ML models into software systems.

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may decline participation, stop participating at any time, or refuse to answer any question without penalty or negative consequence.

3. Purpose of the Study

The general objective of this research is to investigate the current adoption of Software Engineering and Testing principles and practices in AI/ML development contexts and to support the design of a structured development process for AI/ML model development and AI/ML-enabled software systems, grounded in evidence from academic and industrial practice.

Specific objectives include identifying and assessing the adoption of traditional and AI/ML-tailored Software Engineering and Testing principles and practices in academic and industrial contexts, examining how quality-related practices are incorporated into AI/ML development processes, analyzing existing development procedures in academic and industrial scenarios, and supporting the proposal of a more structured and quality-oriented development process for AI/ML-enabled software systems.

4. Participation Procedures

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to complete an online survey questionnaire containing questions about Software Engineering and Testing principles and practices related to the development of AI/ML models and AI/ML-enabled software systems.

The questionnaire is expected to take approximately 20 to 40 minutes. Survey responses will be collected and stored only for the time necessary to conduct data organization, cleaning, analysis, and reporting of research results.

Information about participant location, such as country or region, may be collected only to support analysis of geographic diversity and representation in the study. Study results may be published in academic papers, reports, dissertations, theses, or presentations in aggregated or anonymized form.

5. Risks and Discomforts

All research involving human participants includes some level of risk; however, the risks associated with this study are considered minimal. The study consists exclusively of answering an online survey and does not request directly identifying personal information or sensitive questions about private life, health, beliefs, or unrelated personal matters.

Possible risks include mild discomfort, inconvenience, or fatigue while answering the survey. To reduce these risks, participation is voluntary, you may stop at any time without penalty, you may skip any question you do not wish to answer, and no directly identifying personal information will be requested.

If you leave the survey and return later, you may resume it without penalty from where you stopped. If you drop the survey and do not finish it within 24 hours, you may receive one reminder to return and complete it.

6. Expected Benefits

This study may not provide direct personal benefit to participants. However, it is expected to contribute to a better understanding of how Software Engineering and Testing practices are currently adopted in AI/ML development in both academic and industrial settings.

Expected benefits include generating empirical evidence on the adoption of Software Engineering and Testing practices in AI/ML development, identifying challenges, gaps, and improvement opportunities in current AI/ML development processes, and supporting future design of a more structured and quality-oriented development process for AI/ML models and AI/ML-enabled software systems.

7. Privacy, Confidentiality, and Data Use

All information collected in this survey will be treated confidentially and used exclusively for academic and scientific purposes. The survey does not request your name, the name of your organization, or other directly identifying personal information.

The research team will make every reasonable effort to preserve confidentiality and to present results only in aggregated or anonymized form. Any location-related information, such as country or region, will be used only for diversity and representation analysis.

8. Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal

Your participation in this study is voluntary. You have the right not to participate, to stop participating at any time, and to refuse to answer any question without penalty or negative consequence.

To participate in this study, you must be 18 years of age or older.

9. Costs, Compensation, or Reimbursement

Participation in this survey does not involve any expected financial cost to you, and no payment or financial reward will be provided for participation.

If any exceptional expense directly related to participation in this study occurs, you may contact the research team for clarification and appropriate handling in accordance with the ethical procedures applicable to the study.
